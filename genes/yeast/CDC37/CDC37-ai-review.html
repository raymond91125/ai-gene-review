Gene Description

CDC37 encodes an essential Hsp90 co-chaperone that functions as a kinase-specific adapter, binding to numerous protein kinases and promoting their interaction with the Hsp90 chaperone complex for stabilization and maturation. Cdc37p recognizes and stabilizes nascent kinase polypeptide chains, facilitating their folding through the Hsp90 pathway. It is required for passage through the START phase of the cell cycle and is involved in both the HOG and PKC MAP kinase signaling cascades necessary for adaptation to osmotic and cell wall stress. Cdc37p also contributes to spindle pole body duplication. Orthologous to human CDC37.
